Deep Dish Cookie

Simple, gooey deep-dish cookie recipe. A big, warm cookie baked in a skillet or ramekin. It's crispy on the edges and soft and melty in the middle.

Prep time: 10 minutes

Cook time: 20-25 minutes

Total: 30 minutes

Servings: 2-4 (or 1, because I know your ass is lonely and fat)

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chunks (or chips)
  • (optional) a pinch of sea salt to top

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly butter or spray an 8-inch oven-safe skillet or two 6-oz ramekins.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the melted butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until smooth and glossy.
  3. Add the egg and vanilla extract, whisk until fully combined.
  4. Stir in the flour, baking soda, and salt until just combined — don't overmix.
  5. Fold in your chocolate chips (and any extras like nuts or caramel bits).
  6. Spread the dough evenly in the skillet or ramekins.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the center looks just set. (For a gooier center, bake closer to 20 minutes.)
  8. Let cool for 5-10 minutes — or dig in warm with a scoop of ice cream.

Optional Variations

  • Salted Caramel: Drizzle caramel sauce over the dough before baking.
  • Peanut Butter: Swirl 2 tbsp peanut butter into the top of the dough.
